# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A common phishing attack happens when a user receives an email or message that appears to be from a bank or a well-known company. The message usually creates urgency, such as claiming that the account will be blocked if action is not taken immediately. It contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website which looks identical to the original one. When the user enters their credentials, the attacker captures the information.

Students and working professionals are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms but may not always verify sources.

The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts. Victims may lose money or have their accounts misused for further scams.
